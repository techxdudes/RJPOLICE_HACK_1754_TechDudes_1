About Rajasthan Police

Rajasthan Police is the law enforcement agency of the state of Rajasthan in India. It was established in 1951 and has a strength of over 80,000 personnel. Rajasthan Police is committed to uphold the rule of law, prevent and detect crime, maintain public order, protect the rights and liberties of the people, and ensure peace and harmony in the society.

History of Rajasthan Police

Rajasthan Police has a rich and glorious history that dates back to the medieval period. The origin of Rajasthan Police can be traced to the princely states of Rajasthan, which had their own police forces. After the integration of the princely states into the Indian Union in 1949, the Rajasthan Police Service was formed by merging the police forces of the former states. The first Inspector General of Police of Rajasthan was Shri R. C. Mehta, who took charge on 7 April 1951.

Organization of Rajasthan Police

Rajasthan Police is headed by the Director General of Police (DGP), who is assisted by several Additional Directors General of Police (ADGPs), Inspectors General of Police (IGPs), Deputy Inspectors General of Police (DIGs), Superintendents of Police (SPs), and other officers. Rajasthan Police is divided into 10 police ranges, each headed by an IGP. The police ranges are further divided into 40 police districts, each headed by an SP. The police districts are further divided into police sub-divisions, police circles, police stations, and police outposts.

Achievements of Rajasthan Police

Rajasthan Police has achieved many laurels and accolades for its exemplary performance and service to the nation. Some of the notable achievements of Rajasthan Police are:

  • Rajasthan Police was awarded the President's Police Medal for Gallantry for its role in the Operation Bluestar in 1984.
  • Rajasthan Police was awarded the President's Police Medal for Distinguished Service for its role in the anti-terrorist operations in Punjab in 1988.
  • Rajasthan Police was awarded the Prime Minister's Trophy for the Best State Police for the year 2011-12.
  • Rajasthan Police was awarded the National e-Governance Award for the year 2013-14 for its innovative and citizen-centric initiatives such as Rajasthan Sampark, Abhay Command Centre, and RajCop Citizen App.

Services of Rajasthan Police

Rajasthan Police provides a range of services and facilities to the citizens and visitors of the state. Some of the services of Rajasthan Police are:

  • Online FIR registration and status check
  • Online verification of character, tenant, employee, and vehicle
  • Online payment of traffic challans and e-challans
  • Online application and issuance of police clearance certificate
  • Online registration and renewal of arms license
  • Online reporting and tracking of missing persons and stolen vehicles
  • Online grievance redressal and feedback system
  • Online access to various citizen-centric services and information through RajCop Citizen App
